Hobbies

  • Video Games

    • Primarily I play Massive Multiplayer Online games. Until recently I ran a competitive group for over 4 years. When new content was released we would push to complete it before guides or strategies were released to the public. This led to our group requiring people at not just a high skill level but also with the ability to take in new information and process that into strategies to complete the newest content.

    As the group leader I did all of our recruiting, interviewing, initial vetting of prospective members, and devising novel strategies to test applicants in unknown environments. This came down to ensuring new members fit our group atmosphere, could perform at the level we required, and could contribute in developing strategies.

    For my own personal benchmarks I aimed to be within the top 200 in the world on my class for the entire content release. Each content release contained 5 individual fights with their own rankings. Holding this average ranking meant I had to consistently perform at an incredibly high level across all 5 fights. Several times achieving top 20, top 10, and top 5 rankings. This required keeping up with balance changes on the class, bleeding edge strategies for the fights, and creating new methods to use for my class and team in every encounter.
